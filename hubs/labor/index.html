Prison Labor

Boycott List

Aramark

Used inmates for dining services, facing criticism for poor food quality and accusations of not compensating inmates, leading to a lawsuit in 2019 for using "involuntary servitude."

Avis

Uses inmates to support its call centers and customer service needs, with call centers located in various state and federal prisons across the country.

Compaq (now a subsidiary of Hewlett-Packard)

Has been known to use prison labor for manufacturing circuit boards, a practice discovered over two decades ago with no sign of cessation.​

Fidelity Investments

Has been linked to funding organizations that promote inmate work.

IBM

Circuit boards from the company have come from Texas prisoners.​

JCPenny

Have used inmate labor in Tennessee for making denim products

Kmart

Has used inmate labor in Tennessee for making denim products​.

McDonald’s

The fast-food giant has used prison labor for preparing and packaging various food items, including beef for burgers and potatoes for fries. McDonald's has stated opposition to any form of forced or involuntary prison labor​.

Sprint

The telecommunication company has employed inmates to provide customer service and support through call centers, with statements against involuntary or forced prison labor​.

Starbucks

Has subcontracted coffee packaging jobs to Washington State Prison services, mainly during the holiday season for packaging holiday coffees. Starbucks’ supplier code of conduct emphasizes respecting the rights of individuals who work with them.

Walmart

Inmates have been used for manufacturing tasks, cleaning products of UPC barcodes, and repackaging returned goods. Walmart claims to use voluntary prison labor and pays inmates prevailing wages to prepare them for reintegration by providing marketable skills and job training.

Wendy’s

Similar to McDonald’s, Wendy's has utilized prison labor for processing beef. The company asserts that its suppliers should only use individuals who manufacture voluntarily​.

Whole Foods​

Has sourced some cheese and fish products from prison labor​.

Verizon

The telecommunication company has employed inmates to provide customer service and support through call centers, with statements against involuntary or forced prison labor​.
